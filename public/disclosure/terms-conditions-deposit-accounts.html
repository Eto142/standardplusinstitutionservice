E. Processing / Checks and Withdrawals

Pending Transactions. Transactions which have been authorized, but not yet paid from your account, are Pending Transactions. Pending Transactions are typically POS Transactions where you initiate payment at the point of sale and we authorize it based upon the funds available in your account, but the Transaction may not be presented to us for payment until later. Authorized but not yet paid POS Transactions may remain as Pending Transactions on the account for a period of days.

During the Business Day, the funds allocated for the Pending Transactions (the “allocated funds”) will decrease the amount of funds available in your account. Once allocated, the funds are not available when a request to authorize a different POS (or other) Transaction comes in later in the same Business Day and may result in the later Transaction authorization request being denied or causing an overdraft. During the Business Day you may see Pending Transactions on our Internet Banking Sites. You are encouraged to check the Internet Banking Site frequently to see what funds are available in your account to initiate Transactions.

During overnight processing, after the end of the Business Day (discussed under Processing Order and Posting), the funds previously allocated to all Pending Transactions will be reflected in the funds available to pay the Transactions submitted for payment. These funds may then be used to pay other Transactions, such as Checks or ACH Transactions during posting. Once posting has been completed, the funds for any Pending Transactions that were not presented for payment will be reallocated to the Pending Transaction and will decrease the funds available in your account for the next Business Day.

Due to the allocated funds being available during posting to pay other Transactions, once the Pending Transaction is presented for payment, which may be several days after you initiated it, an overdraft may result if you no longer have sufficient funds available to pay the Pending Transaction. You will not be charged an OD fee if a POS Transaction was authorized when the account had sufficient funds available but caused an overdraft when presented for payment. You will remain responsible for paying any overdraft amount.

POS Transactions may not be authorized for the exact amount of purchase. Examples of this are at gas pumps or some restaurants (where authorization may be prior to tip).

Deposits or Transfers into the account which show as pending will usually be available after final posting has been completed.

Processing Order and Posting. After the end of each Business Day, all deposits, fees and Transactions that have been presented for payment are processed. The process of adding deposits and subtracting Transactions and fees from your account is called “posting”. Transactions may not be posted in the order in which you requested or initiated them. During posting, we will use all funds available in your account to pay the Transactions and fees. Funds available to be used during posting includes any funds that had been allocated during the Business Day to Pending Transactions (discussed in the Pending Transactions section above) as well as any applicable overdraft protection funds (overdraft protection funds include any funds available through Bounce Blocker or the Overdraft Protection Service discussed below). Funds not available to pay a Transaction include funds that are uncollected, subject to any type of hold on the account (other than those related to a Pending Transaction), or a dispute or Legal Process.

First, we will add all of your deposits and incoming Transfers to your account. We will then subtract Checks cashed at our teller window or any other Transaction where cash has already been paid (for example, ATM withdrawals or ATM Transfers). Next, we group your other Transactions and any related fees by type, such as POS, ACH or Checks.

Some fees may be posted at the same time as the related Transaction. Some fees are posted after all other Transactions have been posted. In rare cases, those fees may be posted after the Overdraft Protection Service has been utilized. This could result in an overdraft in your account. You must have signed up for the Overdraft Protection Service Terms for Using Your Savings, Money Market or Checking Account to utilize this overdraft service.

POS Transactions will be posted by the date and time they were received by the Bank from the merchant. The earliest date and time will be subtracted first. The most recent date and time will be subtracted last. The POS Transaction may remain pending for a number of days (see Pending Transactions above) after they were authorized, or the merchant may submit the POS Transaction on the same Business Day it was authorized. The Bank has no control over when the merchant submits the POS Transaction for payment.

ACH Transactions will be posted by the date and time established in the submission to the Bank for payment. The earliest date and time will be subtracted first. The most recent date and time will be subtracted last. In some cases, ACH Transactions may post to your account on the same day they occur but may not post for several days after you initiated the ACH Transaction. The Bank has no control over when the ACH Transaction will be submitted to the Bank for payment or what date will be identified in the submission of the ACH Transaction for payment.

For POS Transactions or ACH Transactions, the date and time the Transaction took place may be affected by the location of the Transaction due to differing time zones. Within each type of Transaction (such as POS or ACH) if more than one Transaction has the same date and time, we will subtract the Transaction with the lowest amount first. Any Transaction without a date and time noted on it when it reaches us will be subtracted within its group after all Transactions with a date and time. The Transaction with the lowest amount will be subtracted first. The Transaction with the highest amount will be subtracted last.

For Checks, the group of Checks, which does not include those where cash has already been paid and the amount already subtracted, will be subtracted in the order of the number on the Check (serial number) with the lowest number posted first. On occasion a Check will be presented without a serial number. If more than one Check without a serial number is received by us on any one day the Checks will be grouped together and will be posted with the lowest amount first and will be posted before those with printed serial numbers. Checks without a serial number will show a serial number of “0” on the account statement.

Once we have paid all possible Transactions using the order described above, we will look for and pay smaller Items utilizing all of the funds available in your account. In limited situations, at our discretion, we may determine to pay larger Items first. This may result in overdraft fees.

All subtractions from your account will first be made from collected funds. Collected funds are those funds we have received from another financial institution in payment for Items deposited to your account. We may refuse to pay a Transaction against uncollected funds in your checking, savings, or money market accounts as set out in Your Ability to Withdraw Funds from Your Deposit Account. We may refuse to pay a Transaction against uncollected funds even if we have allowed them in the past.

After the end of the Business Day, all Transactions go through the same posting process described above. No Transaction will be final until the posting process has occurred. Deposits or Transfers into the account will usually be available after final posting has been completed.
